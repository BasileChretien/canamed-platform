This page is the full privacy policy for the CaNaMED platform (https://canamed-69785.web.app), delivered as a layered notice to satisfy GDPR Article 13 for participants in France and APPI Article 17, 21, 27(5), 28 for participants in Japan. The short summary shown on the join screen is for quick reading; this longer page is the legally binding text. If anything below contradicts the short summary, this text governs.

2. Why we collect your data (purposes)

We use your data for the following purposes only. New purposes require a new consent notice.

  1. Running the live workshop — placing you in a balanced Franco-Japanese room, showing your name and contributions to others in that room, and computing the team scores.
  2. Facilitator debriefing — at the end of the session your facilitator downloads a copy of every group's work (the "archive") for that day's debrief.
  3. Education-research analysis and publication — if you ticked the optional second consent box on the join form, the researchers may include your contributions in aggregated, pseudonymised analyses published in medical-education journals and in PhD theses. If you did not tick that box, your contributions are used only for purpose 1 + 2 and not retained for analysis.

3. Legal basis

Under GDPR:

  • Art. 6(1)(a) — your explicit consent, captured by the two checkboxes on the join form. You can withdraw consent at any time (see §10).
  • Art. 9(2)(a) — for any special-category data you may disclose in free-text answers (health-related, religious, philosophical opinions on opioid use, end-of-life, family disclosure, etc.), we additionally rely on your explicit consent for that category.
  • Where the data is used for the research project (purpose 3 above), we also rely on Art. 9(2)(j) — scientific research with safeguards under Art. 89 (pseudonymisation, restricted access, no individual decisions taken about you).

Under APPI:

  • Art. 17/21 — purposes specified above before acquisition.
  • Art. 20(2) — prior opt-in consent for sensitive personal information (要配慮個人情報) captured via the same consent checkboxes.
  • Art. 27(5) — joint use as described in §1.

4. What we collect, and from whom

  • From you, on the join form: first name (free text), university, year of study, self-rated English (CEFR).
  • From your activity during the workshop: your free-text answers, your votes on team decisions, which clinical findings you revealed, your presence (which room you are in), and the points your team earns or loses.
  • From your device (technical): a random per-tab client ID stored in your browser's sessionStorage, kept only for as long as the tab is open. No cookies, no tracking, no advertising identifiers.
  • From Google, if you sign in: your email address, display name and (optionally) profile picture. You can sign in anonymously instead — Google sign-in is never required.

5. Sensitive personal information

The workshop scenarios cover opioid prescribing, breaking bad news, end-of-life disclosure, and family-vs-autonomy ethics. When you write free-text answers about these scenarios you may disclose your own health-related, religious or philosophical opinions. Under GDPR Art. 9 and APPI Art. 2(3), this is special-category / 要配慮個人情報 and is processed only on your explicit consent (the second checkbox on the join form).

6. Recipients and processors

Your data is held on Google Firebase Realtime Database operated by Google LLC. Google processes the data as our processor under the Firebase Data Processing Addendum and the EU Standard Contractual Clauses.

Within the universities, your data is accessible to the named CaNaMED research team (Caen and Nagoya), your facilitator for the session, and the platform administrator for technical maintenance. No data is shared with anyone else.

7. International transfers

The database is hosted in Belgium (Google europe-west1 region), within the European Economic Area. For French participants this is intra-EEA processing under GDPR.

For Japanese participants, this is a cross-border transfer under APPI Article 28. The European Union has been designated by Japan's Personal Information Protection Commission (PPC) as a jurisdiction providing protection at a level equivalent to Japan under the 2019 EU–Japan mutual adequacy decision. We hold an internal record of this transfer under APPI Art. 28(3).

If you sign in with Google, your identity-token traffic is also processed by Google LLC entities in the United States, under EU Standard Contractual Clauses and Google's published technical and organisational measures. We hold a Transfer Impact Assessment on file referencing those measures.

8. Retention

  • Live session data (your name in sessions/{code}/pool, presence, typing indicators) is purged from the live database within 7 days of the workshop ending.
  • The session archive JSON (downloaded by the facilitator at session end) is pseudonymised within 30 days — names replaced by codes — and the linkage table is destroyed within 6 months. The identified copy is destroyed at the same time.
  • If you ticked the second consent box for research use, the pseudonymised dataset is retained for up to 5 years after the related publication, then irreversibly deleted.
  • If you signed in with Google, your profile and history under users/{uid} are retained until you delete your account from the "My account" panel.

9. Your rights

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you (GDPR Art. 15, APPI Art. 33).
  • Have inaccurate data corrected (Art. 16 / Art. 34).
  • Have your data deleted (Art. 17 / Art. 35).
  • Restrict processing (Art. 18).
  • Receive your data in a portable format (Art. 20).
  • Object to processing (Art. 21).
  • Withdraw your consent at any time, with effect for the future (Art. 7(3); APPI Art. 38(3)).

To exercise any of these, write to canamed-ethics@unicaen.fr (stable contact mailbox for data-subject rights, monitored by the joint controllers). We acknowledge requests within 5 working days and respond within 30 days (GDPR Art. 12(3); APPI Art. 38).

Exercising any right has no effect on your grades, your standing at your university, or your participation in the workshop. There is no penalty for refusing or withdrawing.

10. Withdrawing consent / leaving the workshop

You can withdraw consent for research use at any time. After the workshop, write to the email address in §9 with your session code and your name — we will redact your contributions from the pseudonymised research archive and confirm in writing. Withdrawal does not retroactively affect data already used in a published analysis if it cannot be unpublished, but no new analyses will include your data.

During the workshop, you can press Leave at any time to exit. Your typing buffer and presence are removed immediately; your already-submitted contributions remain in the room's record for the facilitator's debriefing copy unless you also request deletion using the contact above.

11. Right to complain

If you believe your rights have been violated, you can complain to your supervisory authority:

  • France (CNIL): www.cnil.fr/en/plaintes
  • Japan (PPC, 個人情報保護委員会): www.ppc.go.jp, 〒100-0013 東京都千代田区霞が関3-2-1

12. Automated decision-making

The platform automatically awards or deducts points based on the content of your team's typed answers and on the clinical choices your team makes (keyword scoring + penalty list). These scores are used only for in-session pedagogy — they do not contribute to your university grade and have no effect on your record. You can flag any score you disagree with to your facilitator during the session for human review.

There is no profiling, no automated decision producing legal or similarly significant effects on you (GDPR Art. 22; APPI Art. 26-3).

13. Security

Personal data is stored over HTTPS, in a Firebase Realtime Database governed by access-control rules that restrict reads and writes to participants in the same session. Privileged session fields (admin password hash, scenario, session creation marker) are immutable once a session is created. Session passwords are stored as PBKDF2-SHA256 hashes (100,000 iterations, salted with the session code). The service-account key used for automated deployment is held as a GitHub Actions secret and scoped to Firebase Hosting / Realtime Database admin only.

Despite these measures, breach of the platform is possible. In the event of a breach affecting your data:

  • Under GDPR Art. 33–34, the controller will notify the CNIL within 72 hours and you directly if the breach is likely to result in a high risk to your rights.
  • Under APPI Art. 26 (2022 amendment), the operator will file a preliminary report to the PPC within 3–5 days and a final report within 30 days, and notify each affected data subject.

14. Children

The platform is intended for adult medical students aged 18 and older. If you are under 16 (GDPR Art. 8) or under 20 (Japanese university norms for younger undergraduates), please contact your facilitator before joining; additional parental-consent procedures may be required.

17. Local data on your device

Your browser stores small pieces of data on your device to make the platform work:

  • sessionStorage: a random per-tab client identifier (canamed_client), cleared when the tab closes.
  • localStorage: the session code you are unlocked into (canamed_session), your name and join details for auto-resume (canamed_resume), your preference for celebration sounds (canamed_sound), your chosen UI language (canamed_lang), and — for facilitators only — the last workshop's setup so it can be cloned in one click (canamed_last_workshop: label, scenario id, room count, Teams/questionnaire links — never any password). Most of these are cleared when you press the Leave button or the Return to CANAMED button on the closed-session screen; the language and clone-stash persist until you clear them.
  • sessionStorage (per browser tab, wiped when the tab closes): a small in-page error-log buffer (up to 50 entries) capturing uncaught JavaScript exceptions and Content-Security-Policy violations. The buffer never leaves your browser. A facilitator can export it manually via the admin dashboard's Download error log button if you report a bug; otherwise it is dropped when the tab closes.

No third-party cookies, no tracking pixels, no advertising identifiers are loaded by the platform.

Third-party services loaded by the page

To make the platform work, your browser also loads a small number of third-party scripts and resources. None of these receive your name, university, free-text answers, or any other content you typed.

  • Google Firebase SDKs (auth, realtime database, App Check, performance) — from www.gstatic.com, loaded with SubResource Integrity hashes so a compromised CDN cannot swap them. These are what let you sign in (if you use Google sign-in) and synchronise the live workshop state with the other participants in your room.
  • reCAPTCHA v3 (Classic) — Google's bot- detection service, loaded from www.google.com via Firebase App Check using the classic (free-tier) v3 provider. It receives only a token request from your browser to produce a per-request attestation that you are a real user; it does not see your name, university, answers, or any workshop content. Google's data-handling for reCAPTCHA is governed by their Google Privacy Policy.
  • Firebase Performance Monitoring — when enabled by the deployment operator (see CANAMED_PERF_MONITORING in firebase-config.js), the Firebase SDK sends anonymous timing data to Google: page load, first contentful paint, time-to-first-byte, and the duration / status code of each network request issued by the page. No participant text, name, university, consent value, session code, or clientId is sent. The data is used to monitor real-user performance (e.g. comparing platform responsiveness between cohorts). It is governed by the Firebase Privacy and Security page and the Google Privacy Policy.
  • QR code rendering — generated entirely client-side via a vendored copy of qrcode.js; no outbound request is made. The QR contains only the public session-join URL.
